What is Halloween?

Halloween is a holiday celebrated in many countries on October 31st. It originated from the ancient Celtic festival of Samhain, which was believed to be a time when the boundary between the living and the dead was thinnest. People would dress up in costumes and light bonfires to ward off evil spirits. Today, Halloween is celebrated with traditions like carving pumpkins, trick-or-treating, and attending themed parties. It is often associated with spooky and supernatural themes, including ghosts, witches, and monsters.

Halloween Activities

Halloween activities are a fun way to celebrate the spooky season. Trick-or-treating is a classic activity for kids, while teenagers and adults may enjoy haunted houses or Halloween parties. Pumpkin carving and decorating your home with spooky decorations are also popular activities. For those who prefer a low-key celebration, watching Halloween movies or reading scary stories can be a fun way to get in the Halloween spirit. Other popular activities include apple bobbing, making caramel apples, and attending costume contests. No matter how you celebrate, Halloween offers a range of activities for all ages.
